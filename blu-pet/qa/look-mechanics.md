# Blu look mechanics

Blu is a soft, long-haired Ragdoll with very large physical blue eyes, a distinct head on a seated body, mobile ears, and no props. Her paws, haunches, lower torso, scale, and baseline stay anchored. The blue eye globes lead each gaze; eyelids reshape subtly, then her muzzle, head, ears, neck ruff, and upper chest follow by a restrained natural turn or pitch. The torso must not rotate as a whole. Fur and ears follow smoothly with slight lag while preserving her seal-point mask and face proportions.

Motion budget: each 22.5-degree step changes eye aim, nose position, head angle, ear occlusion, and upper-ruff silhouette by a small even amount. No neighboring pair may jump in scale, baseline, head size, or lower-body registration.

- 000 up: eyes and muzzle pitch upward; chin and neck ruff become slightly more visible; ears remain symmetric and paws stay fixed.
- 090 screen-right: pupils, nose, and muzzle shift unmistakably toward screen-right; Blu's screen-left cheek/ruff becomes more visible while the far cheek and ear become partly occluded.
- 180 down: eyelids lower around downward-aimed eyes; muzzle and chin tuck toward the chest; ear tops remain visible while the forehead pitches forward.
- 270 screen-left: pupils, nose, and muzzle shift unmistakably toward screen-left; Blu's screen-right cheek/ruff becomes more visible while the far cheek and ear become partly occluded.

Diagonals interpolate these pose families evenly. Preserve the original blue eye construction: rotate/redraw the complete eye surfaces and eyelids together, never paste sliding pupils or replacement googly eyes. The final 337.5 pose must sit one small step before 000, and 157.5 one small step before 180.
